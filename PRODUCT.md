# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Back on Track is for older adults with ongoing low back pain who avoid movement because they worry it will cause harm. Many users may have reduced vision, reduced fine motor control, lower health literacy, low confidence with phone apps, and a strong need to remain independent at home.

Secondary users are physiotherapists, tutors, family members, and caregivers who need to trust the toolkit quickly and explain it without extra setup.

## Product Purpose

The product helps a screened patient do small daily movements, understand pain without fear, follow a bad-day plan, remember a personal goal, track simple progress, and recognize when medical help is needed.

Success means the app feels safe, calm, legible, and worth returning to on both a phone and a desktop browser.

## Brand Personality

Warm, practical, steady.

The product should feel like a thoughtful clinic handout made interactive, not a medical portal, wellness app, or generic template.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like:

- A hospital admin system.
- A generic wellness dashboard.
- A marketing landing page.
- A decorative card grid.
- A neon health startup.
- A phone mockup trapped inside a desktop browser.

## Design Principles

- Respect low energy: keep choices obvious and actions close to the user's current task.
- Show safety through craft: polished spacing, high contrast, and stable controls build trust.
- Make the physical toolkit visible: the app and magnets should feel like the same system.
- Use desktop space honestly: a Windows Chrome view should become a wider care desk, not an enlarged phone.
- Keep clinical boundaries clear: support self-management without pretending to diagnose or treat.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA or better. Use large tap targets, plain language, visible focus states, reduced-motion support, strong contrast, and layouts that do not depend on color alone. Avoid fragile text fitting and keep core tasks comfortable at narrow phone widths and desktop browser widths.
